The CA in Your Pocket That Does Not Exist
BACKGROUND
India has 1.4 crore GST-registered MSMEs. A kirana store owner in Lucknow receives 500 to 2,000
invoices per month via WhatsApp images, printed digital invoices, machine-generated receipts, and
scanned PDFs. They currently pay 15,000 to 40,000 rupees per month to a chartered accountant who
manually enters this data into GST portals. The CA's real value is not data entry. It is knowing that a
specific invoice carries the wrong HSN code and that fixing it will unlock 2,400 rupees in blocked
Input Tax Credit this month.
THE PROBLEM
The GST portal offers no diagnostic tool that explains what is mismatched, why it matters, and what
action to take in plain language. A small trader who tries to file independently misses ITC claims they
are legally entitled to and loses real money every month. The core issue is an unstructured data
paradox coupled with a language barrier. The reality of a small business is physical and messy while
government compliance is digital and rigidly structured. Traders lack a bridge to translate their daily
commercial artifacts into actionable compliance realities without paying a professional gatekeeper.
